Bus Guide for Beginners

1

Finding Your Bus

Check bus stop codes and use apps like Singapore Bus Guide or Google Maps to find which buses go to your destination.

2

Boarding the Bus

Board from the front, tap your card on entry, and remember to tap out when you exit to avoid overcharging.

3

Getting Around Republic Poly

Common buses to RP: 856, 859, 969. Nearest MRT: Woodlands (NS9) with connecting buses.

Essential Travel Tips

Payment Methods

Get an EZ-Link card or SimplyGo card. You can also use contactless credit/debit cards for seamless travel.

Peak Hours

Avoid 7-9 AM and 5-7 PM on weekdays when trains and buses are crowded. Plan your journey accordingly.

Travel Etiquette

Stand on the left of escalators, give up seats for elderly/pregnant passengers, and avoid eating/drinking.

Useful Apps

Download MyTransport.SG, Google Maps, and Grab for real-time info and alternative transport options.

Emergency Info

Emergency button on trains, helplines on station platforms. Keep important numbers saved on your phone.

Save Money

Off-peak travel is cheaper. Monthly passes available for frequent travelers. Top up cards at 7-Eleven stores.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get from Changi Airport to Republic Polytechnic?

Take the MRT from Changi Airport (EW/CG line) towards Joo Koon, change at Tanah Merah to Joo Koon direction, then at Jurong East switch to North South Line towards Woodlands. Get off at Woodlands station and take bus 856, 859, or 969 to Republic Polytechnic.

What's the best card for public transport?

EZ-Link card is recommended for students. It costs S$12 (including S$7 stored value) and can be purchased at MRT stations, 7-Eleven stores, and Cheers outlets. You can also use contactless credit/debit cards with SimplyGo.

Are there student discounts for public transport?

Student concession cards are available for full-time students at approved institutions. Check with Republic Polytechnic's student services for eligibility and application process.

What if I forget to tap out?

You'll be charged the maximum fare from your entry point. To get a refund, visit the TransitLink Ticket Office at major MRT stations within 14 days with your card and explain the situation.

Is public transport safe at night?

Yes, Singapore's public transport is generally very safe at all hours. Trains run until midnight, and night buses operate after that. Stay in well-lit areas and remain aware of your surroundings.
